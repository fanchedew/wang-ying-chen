Reinforcement Learning for Driving Policy Generalization via Risk-Aware Expert Policy and Conditional Diffusion

Guofa Li¹², Yingchen Wang², Delin Ouyang², Jie Li², Xiangyun Ren¹
¹ State Key Laboratory of Intelligent Vehicle Safety Technology, Chongqing Chang'an Automobile Co., Ltd.
² College of Mechanical and Vehicle Engineering, Chongqing University
📑 Download PDF

Abstract

In real-world autonomous driving applications, reinforcement learning (RL) faces several persistent challenges including limited critical training data, slow exploration efficiency, and poor policy generalization. These limitations often result in slow convergence or stagnation in local optima, undermining the safety and generalization of intelligent vehicles. To address these issues, we propose a generation-enhanced RL framework for challenging decision-making scenarios, comprising three stages: data acquisition, data utilization, and data generation. Firstly, a risk-aware expert policy is developed to guide exploration on regions proximal to the boundary between safe and unsafe actions. Secondly, a hierarchical dynamic prioritized replay mechanism dynamically ranks experience samples based on their relevance to current policy updates. Thirdly, a conditional generation method based on diffusion model synthesizes diverse and structurally relevant transitions concentrated near the policy decision boundary. Experimental results demonstrate that our method significantly accelerates policy learning and improves driving performance under complex scenarios.

Key Contributions

  • A risk-aware expert policy that guides exploration toward safety-critical regions near the boundary between safe and unsafe actions during early training phases
  • A hierarchical dynamic prioritized replay mechanism that ranks experience samples by relevance to current policy updates, improving utilization of critical transitions
  • A conditional diffusion model that generates diverse transitions near the policy decision boundary to supplement the replay buffer and cover distributional gaps
